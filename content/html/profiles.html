Key Practitioners

Josef Müller-Brockmann

Swiss graphic designer and pioneer of the International Typographic Style. His grid-based layouts and typographic clarity laid the foundation for Metro’s structure.

Notable Work: “Musica Viva” poster series

Massimo Vignelli

Designer of the Milan Metro and co-creator of the Washington Metro signage system. Advocated for timeless, modular design that influenced Microsoft’s use of typography as navigation.

Notable Work: NYC Subway Map, Washington Metro signage

Harry Weese

Architect of the Washington Metro’s iconic vaulted stations. His brutalist design emphasized spatial rhythm and civic pride—principles echoed in Metro UI’s structure.

Notable Work: Washington Metro architecture

Albert Shum

Led Microsoft’s Windows Phone design team. Formalized Metro’s principles: content over chrome, motion as meaning, and bold typography.

Notable Work: Zune UI, Windows Phone 7, early Fluent Design

Nille Sørensen

Designer of Copenhagen’s Cityringen Metro stations. His work emphasized modularity, local materiality, and intuitive flow—core to Metro’s design ethos.

Notable Work: Cityringen Metro (Copenhagen)
